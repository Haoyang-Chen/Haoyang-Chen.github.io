About

My research interest lies in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Human-Centered Computing, Social Computing, Human Behavior Modeling and cross-disciplines of transportation informatics and health informatics. My goal is to bridge the gap between technological expertise and user- friendly experience by leveraging advanced technologies and creating solutions aligned with user preferences. I am now working at Ubicomp Lab in National University of Singapore as an Research Assistant with Prof. Brian lim. Before joining NUS, I spent 2 years as a Research Assistant at COOLA Lab in Southeast University, where I worked with Prof. Yan LYU and Prof. Wanyuan Wang

Research Projects

Modularized Interpretable MDSS with Visual Programming
Overview:
Designing visual programming toolkit to help physicians build customized diagnosis model.
Combining first-order-logic with neural networks to convert flowcharts into trainable models.
Modularized structure offers step-by-step visualization and interpretation.
Personalized Vehicle Repositioning for Ride-hailing Platforms
Overview:
Model driver cruising preference with a light-weighted LSTM.
Carried out on-field User Study on 106 professional drivers to model driver decision.
Used dual-agent Reinforcement Learning framework to satisfy driver preference and demand-supply balance.
Ear Motion Tracking System for VR Devices
Overview:
Built a prototype using headset and endoscopes to capture inner-ear video.
Used Lucas-Kanade optical flow method to detect ear motion as input to VR device.
Carried out small-scale User Study on 15 volunteers.
